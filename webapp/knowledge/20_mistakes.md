# 20 mistakes not to make when starting YouTube automation

1. **Starting without a niche wedge** — “general interest” channels die. Pick one searchable promise viewers already binge.
2. **Copying packaging without studying retention** — thumbnails and titles matter only if the first 30 seconds keep people.
3. **Publishing before you can ship weekly** — consistency beats perfection. Automate only after the format is repeatable.
4. **Scripting like a blog post** — YouTube is spoken. Short sentences, hooks, pattern interrupts, one idea per beat.
5. **Ignoring the hook** — cold open with a payoff promise. Never start with channel intros or soft context dumps.
6. **Over-automating the wrong step** — AI for B-roll/voice is leverage; AI for niche choice without judgment is roulette.
7. **Using stock footage as the product** — generic B-roll signals low effort. Match visuals to specific nouns in the script.
8. **Voice that sounds like a robot reading** — pacing, emphasis, and a consistent host voice matter more than “perfect” TTS.
9. **No visual system** — stick to one recipe (explainer, cinematic, avatar). Mixing styles breaks brand recognition.
10. **Writing captions that fight the picture** — big on-screen text over busy scenes kills watch time.
11. **Skipping competitor teardown** — watch top videos with notebook: structure, pacing, CTAs, end screens.
12. **Chasing every trend** — trends amplify a proven format; they don’t invent one.
13. **Measuring vanity metrics first** — track CTR, AVD%, returning viewers, and RPM before subscriber vanity.
14. **Uploading without packaging tests** — title/thumbnail are half the job. Iterate those before rewriting full scripts.
15. **No upload kit** — description, tags, chapters, and end-screen plan should ship with the video, not after.
16. **Burning credits on untested length** — prove retention on shorter pieces before cooking 20-minute epics.
17. **Ignoring rights and voice consent** — clone only voices you own or have written permission to use.
18. **Building in public before product-market fit** — quiet shipping until the format converts; then amplify.
19. **Treating tools as strategy** — ChannelRecipe cooks videos; you still own niche, promise, and editorial judgment.
20. **Quitting at video 8** — most automation channels need a batch of publishes before the algorithm or you learns.
